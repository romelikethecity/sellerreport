Sales Manager Salary in Chicago 2026: Base, OTE, Quota

By Rome Thorndike · 2026-05-15 · 466 words

Sales Manager roles in Chicago pay a median base salary of $165K in 2026, with on-target earnings (OTE) at approximately $235K for reps at full quota attainment. The full range spans $135K to $201K on base salary depending on company stage, segment, and rep experience.

Sales Manager in Chicago at a glance: Median base $165K. Median OTE $235K. Typical split: 70/30 base/variable. Quota: $4M-$10M team annual ARR.

How Chicago compares to the national median

The US national median for Sales Manager roles is $150K. Chicago pay runs 10% above the national figure. Chicago combines enterprise SaaS, financial services, and B2B services sales. Compensation runs near the US median but cost of living below SF, NYC, and Boston produces strong real-income outcomes.

Cost-of-living adjustment

Chicago has a cost-of-living index of 107 versus the US average of 100. After cost-of-living adjustment, a $165K Sales Manager salary in Chicago converts to approximately $154K in national-equivalent purchasing power. Sales professionals weighing offers across cities should compare cost-adjusted pay rather than nominal salary.

Role context: Sales Manager

First-line sales managers run 5-12 AE teams. Carry team quota plus individual contributor responsibility in player-coach roles at smaller orgs. In Chicago, Sales Manager compensation typically follows a 70/30 base/variable base-to-variable split with quota expectations of $4M-$10M team annual ARR. Strong performers earn 110-150% of OTE through accelerators above plan.

What drives the high and low ends

The high end of the Chicago Sales Manager range ($201K base) is concentrated at enterprise SaaS, financial services, and infrastructure vendors with strong product-market fit. The low end ($135K base) shows up at early-stage startups, SMB-focused vendors, and roles with heavier variable compensation built into the OTE structure.

Equity matters more in Chicago than in lower-cost metros. Roughly 60-75% of senior Sales Manager roles in Chicago include equity grants. Public-company RSU packages typically run $20K-$80K per year for AE roles and higher for management roles.
