Sales Manager Salary in New York 2026: Base, OTE, Quota

By Rome Thorndike · 2026-05-15 · 481 words

Sales Manager roles in New York pay a median base salary of $180K in 2026, with on-target earnings (OTE) at approximately $257K for reps at full quota attainment. The full range spans $147K to $219K on base salary depending on company stage, segment, and rep experience.

Sales Manager in New York at a glance: Median base $180K. Median OTE $257K. Typical split: 70/30 base/variable. Quota: $4M-$10M team annual ARR.

How New York compares to the national median

The US national median for Sales Manager roles is $150K. New York pay runs 20% above the national figure. New York sales compensation reflects the city's mix of financial services, enterprise SaaS, and AdTech. Enterprise AE roles in fintech and infrastructure software cluster at the high end.

Cost-of-living adjustment

New York has a cost-of-living index of 168 versus the US average of 100. After cost-of-living adjustment, a $180K Sales Manager salary in New York converts to approximately $107K in national-equivalent purchasing power. Sales professionals weighing offers across cities should compare cost-adjusted pay rather than nominal salary.

Role context: Sales Manager

First-line sales managers run 5-12 AE teams. Carry team quota plus individual contributor responsibility in player-coach roles at smaller orgs. In New York, Sales Manager compensation typically follows a 70/30 base/variable base-to-variable split with quota expectations of $4M-$10M team annual ARR. Strong performers earn 110-150% of OTE through accelerators above plan.

What drives the high and low ends

The high end of the New York Sales Manager range ($219K base) is concentrated at enterprise SaaS, financial services, and infrastructure vendors with strong product-market fit. The low end ($147K base) shows up at early-stage startups, SMB-focused vendors, and roles with heavier variable compensation built into the OTE structure.

Equity matters more in New York than in lower-cost metros. Roughly 60-75% of senior Sales Manager roles in New York include equity grants. Public-company RSU packages typically run $20K-$80K per year for AE roles and higher for management roles.
