Cognism vs Lusha: Side-by-Side 2026 Sales Comparison

By Rome Thorndike · 2026-05-15 · 883 words

Key Takeaways

  • Cognism uses custom pricing and targets Sales teams selling into EMEA that need GDPR-compliant phone-verified data.
  • Lusha starts at $29 and targets Individual reps and small teams that need fast direct dial lookups.
  • Cognism's standout feature is Diamond Data phone-verified contacts and Do Not Call list scrubbing; Lusha leads with Chrome extension with direct dial pull from LinkedIn profiles.

Cognism and Lusha are common evaluations for B2B sales teams in B2B contact data with European coverage. This is the practical comparison: where each tool wins, where each tool loses, and which team profile fits each pick. Data is drawn from public vendor pricing, product docs, G2 reviews, and our 2026 sales hiring dataset of 4,494 postings.

Verdict: Cognism is an enterprise data platform. Lusha is a Chrome extension. Different jobs.

Where Cognism wins

Best for: EMEA SDR teams that need phone-verified contacts at scale.

Strongest EMEA dataset in the market with disciplined compliance. Picked by teams running outbound into the UK, DACH, France, and the Nordics.

The data point that matters: Cognism starts at Custom (typically $1,500+ per user/yr). Diamond Data phone-verified contacts and Do Not Call list scrubbing. The deal-breaker pattern shows up in: North American coverage is thinner than Apollo or ZoomInfo.

Adoption signal from our 2026 hiring dataset: tools in b2b data with emea coverage appear in hundreds of B2B sales job postings, with the strongest concentration at SaaS, security, and infrastructure vendors. Sellers evaluating Cognism usually compare it against the alternative covered here plus 2-3 other options before committing.

Where Lusha wins

Best for: Individual reps and small teams doing LinkedIn-led prospecting.

The fastest way to grab a direct dial off a LinkedIn profile. Strong for low-volume rep workflows. Weak for list building or complex filters.

The data point that matters: Lusha starts at Free / $29+ per user/mo. Chrome extension with direct dial pull from LinkedIn profiles. The deal-breaker pattern shows up in: Database depth lags Apollo and ZoomInfo outside North America.

Adoption signal: Lusha shows up most often in job postings for individual reps and small teams that need fast direct dial lookups. The integration footprint includes Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, Pipedrive, which determines how smoothly it slots into an existing tech stack.

The full verdict

Cognism is built for SDR teams selling into EMEA at scale, with phone-verified Diamond Data, advanced filtering, and Salesforce or HubSpot sync. Lusha is built for individual reps who want a one-click way to grab direct dials and emails off LinkedIn profiles. Different jobs. Most teams that compare them are asking whether they need a platform or a lookup tool. If you build lists of 500+ contacts at a time and run multi-channel outbound, Cognism. If you research one prospect at a time and never export a list, Lusha.

Pricing breakdown

Cognism $1,500-2,500/seat/yr. Lusha $0 free to $79/seat/mo Premium.

Data-platform total cost of ownership runs 15-25% above per-seat list price once you add data hygiene workflows, CRM sync premium tiers, and the rep-time cost of curating exports. At 50-rep scale that translates to $17,400-$900,000 in list-price seat cost per year before the discount you negotiate. Enterprise deployments at this scale typically need 90-150 days from contract signature to full team productivity. Plan for 2-3 dedicated admin FTE-weeks and 8-12 hours of training per rep across the first quarter.

Procurement note: at enterprise scale, both Cognism and Lusha bake in security review, MSA negotiation, and data-processing agreements as separate workstreams. Plan 30-60 days for legal review on a clean contract, and 90+ days when red-line counter-proposals begin.

Implementation effort

Cognism implementation is fast on paper (CRM connector setup runs 7-21 days) but the real work is hygiene. Plan for 30-60 days of data-cleanup and enrichment-rule design before SDRs can run automated workflows against the database without producing bounce-heavy lists.

Lusha go-lives often stall at the bulk-export-to-Salesforce step. The data is good; the field mappings are wrong. Resolve the schema mapping in a single half-day workshop before any rep runs a live sequence against the imported list.

Whether the team picks Cognism or Lusha, the reps that hit quota are the ones whose managers run weekly deal inspections inside the tool. Teams that buy Cognism-class platforms but coach out of spreadsheets get the same outcome as teams that bought nothing.
